Market Dynamics & Pipeline Pressure

Marrakech recorded ~4M visitors and 12M overnight stays in 2024, with city-wide occupancy above 72% and luxury supply growing 22% YoY. H1 2025 occupancy held at 71%. Lodging Econometrics counts 25 projects / 4,586 rooms in pipeline, anchored by the Rixos/Alliances ~$300M two-resort programme and Kimpton (67 keys, Q4 2026).

Market gap. The market bifurcates between commodity all-inclusive (Palmeraie) and ultra-luxury palaces (medina/Hivernage). No established design-led upper-upscale lifestyle resort sits between these poles in the Ennakhil corridor.

Commercial Terms Assumptions

Boutique-international HMA assumptions, flagged for replacement with Kerten standard term sheet. Base management fee 3.0–4.0% gross revenue · IMF 8–12% Adjusted GOP above 10% priority return · S&M 1.0–1.5% · TSA $500K–1.5M or 1–3% hard cost · Term 10–15 years + 5-year renewals · No key money expected · No reservation/loyalty fee.

Morocco-specific: 10% WHT on gross fees to non-resident operator (UAE–Morocco treaty relief to verify); 10% VAT accommodation; tourism-sector full IS exemption first 5 years.
