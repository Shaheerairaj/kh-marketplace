A Concept Brief for The House Hotel

Ennakhil
Resort

228 keys at the threshold where Marrakech ends and the Atlas begins — a contemporary estate built for arrivals that feel like discoveries.

Marrakech, Morocco · Management Contract · Greenfield · FY2027

A market
that bifurcates,
and a corridor
still unclaimed.

Marrakech recorded roughly four million visitors and twelve million overnight stays in 2024, with city-wide occupancy above 72 percent and luxury supply growing 22 percent year-on-year. The pipeline is heavy — Lodging Econometrics counts twenty-five projects and 4,586 rooms in the city alone — and yet the structure of the market remains stubbornly bipolar.

At one pole sit the commodity all-inclusive resorts of the Palmeraie corridor: Riu, Be Live, Barceló, drawing volume leisure at $120 to $200 a night. At the other, the ultra-luxury palaces of the medina and Hivernage — Royal Mansour, La Mamounia, Amanjena — set the ceiling at $1,300 and above. Between these poles, in the eastern Ennakhil corridor, no established design-led upper-upscale lifestyle resort exists.

Kimpton's 67-key arrival in the southwest corridor in late 2026 is the only signal that other operators see this gap. It is on a different axis. The first-mover advantage in the eastern corridor is time-sensitive but currently unclaimed.

Owner Snapshot

LRHN — Les Résidences Hôtelières du Nord. Active multi-tier Hilton developer in northern Morocco. Confirmed pipeline: Tapestry Chefchaouen (100 keys, 2028) and Hilton Garden Inn Tetouan (97 keys, 2029).

The Marrakech project marks geographic expansion outside the northern footprint. Engagement of In Extenso TCH (Paris) for an open operator selection signals that LRHN is not pre-committed to Hilton on this asset.

The site itself is a five-hectare plot at the junction of the route de Fès and the route d'Ouarzazate, twelve kilometres from the city centre, with unobstructed views toward the High Atlas and a documented amber dust-light at dawn and dusk. Five hundred metres from the Marrakech palm grove, adjacent to a sixteen-hectare luxury development whose brand affiliation has not yet been disclosed.

The thesis writes itself, but only just. The House Hotel's design-led understated contemporary lux is not a brand description — it is the only credible answer to a corridor where palace nostalgia would feel out of place and all-inclusive efficiency would forfeit the view.

Built for arrivals that feel like discoveries, days that orient themselves to the mountain horizon, and a kind of unhurried Moroccan modernism that the medina hotels cannot give and the all-inclusive resorts have never tried.
— The Vision Statement

II · The Numbers

The In Extenso feasibility study targets a stabilised FY2030 ADR of 1,709 MAD — roughly $170 — with 74 percent occupancy and a revenue mix of 52 percent rooms, 40 percent food and beverage, 8 percent other. The food and beverage share runs above the 20 to 25 percent global hotel norm, but is plausible for a design-led suburban resort with destination outlets and a captive twelve-kilometre radius. CBRE's 2025 hotel research confirms resort F&B is leading industry growth.

The arithmetic is plausible only with a defensible brand position. Twenty-five new projects in the city pipeline will harden the existing tiers, not challenge them. The premium The House Hotel commands — over Riu in the corridor, beneath Amanjena on the same N9 axis — is a function of identity, not square metres.

Pillars · Four Anchors

  • Atlas Threshold — public spaces oriented to the mountain horizon and amber dust-light.
  • Living Maker Souk — a working craft village within the property; rotating Marrakech artisans.
  • Garden as Story — five hectares as a single landscape composition; gardens as circulation.
  • Resort MICE Pavilion — 500–600 m² ballroom; the only lifestyle alternative to medina palaces.

What the brief proposes — and what the corridor will reward — is a property that reads as a cultural argument as much as a commercial one. A material palette anchored in Amazigh, not Arab-Andalusian. Pisé walls, tadelakt finishes, hanbel weavings from named Ourika cooperatives, Tamegroute green-glaze ceramics. Architecture as desert modernism, not riad nostalgia.

The Living Maker Souk is the programmatic counterpart to the material palette: a working studio cluster within the resort's garden zone where rotating artisans produce pieces incorporated into the hotel and available for purchase. Quarterly artisan rotations create a returning-guest loop, and a partnership corridor with the Musée d'Art Contemporain Africain Al Maaden — eight kilometres further south on the same N9 axis — supplies the cultural infrastructure no other non-medina hotel can structurally claim.

Each space carries one subtle Moroccan gesture, thoughtfully integrated rather than decorative. No lantern clichés. No heavy mosaics.
— THH Marrakech Brand Precedent

Risk Roughly fifteen to twenty percent of African pipeline historically slips twelve months or more. A buffer should be built in. If opening slips to FY2028, stabilisation moves to FY2031 — beyond the World Cup peak — a material underwriting consideration.

Open Ten questions for the client remain on the table, foremost among them Kerten's actual standard HMA terms (the Commercial Terms section uses boutique-international industry norms that must be replaced before commercial proposal stage), the operator-level position on all-inclusive within The House Hotel brand, and the UAE–Morocco withholding tax treaty position on management fees.

None of these are obstacles. They are the working agenda of a deal that, by every available signal, is structured to be done.
