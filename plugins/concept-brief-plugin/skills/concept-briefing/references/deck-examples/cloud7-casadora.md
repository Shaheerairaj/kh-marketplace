The Art of Spontaneous Living

THE VISION

To create a distinctive seaside destination in El Jadida
that celebrates Moroccan heritage, modern comfort,
and a sense of belonging - where guests and owners
experience authentic connections, relaxation, and a
vibrant coastal lifestyle.

THE MISSION

Elevate the guests’ living and hospitality journey above the
conventional offering in El Jadida by:
•

Curating experiences rooted in the authenticity of the
place and community;

•

Building a resort that serves as both a sanctuary and a
social hub for guests, residents, and locals alike;

•

Delivering purpose-driven hospitality, multisensory guest
programming paired with thoughtful modern design;

•

Ensuring profitability and the assets’ value through
efficient operations and strong brand positioning.

FARM-TO-TABLE RESTAURANT

Joontos will be the resort’s main all-day dining destination.

Inspired by a food-sharing concept, ‘Joontos’ - derived
from the Spanish word ‘Juntos,’ meaning ‘together’ perfectly captures the spirit of sharing.

ingredients sourced directly from the resort’s own farm (this

Moroccan gastronomy.

HIGH-LEVEL MASTERPLAN RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the currently limited information and available floorplans, we propose the following layout considerations to better align
with the resort’s program and operational needs. These are preliminary and non-exhaustive suggestions and will require further
refinement as the development progresses.

Resort Masterplan
• Padel Courts: Move the padel courts to the multi-sport courts area to create a consolidated fitness zone.
• Organic Garden & Farm: Convert the former padel court area into an organic garden and farm, which will function as supply for
Joontos’ Farm-to-Table concept, as well as an attraction for the kids club.
• Technical Areas: Technical systems, such as water tanks, central pump stations, STPs, transformers, generators etc., could be placed
in the former padel courts areas. Details on design and space allocation will be shared moving forward.

• Bowling: We would not suggest implementing this area, since an indoor activity does not align with guest expectations or the
resort’s outdoor, lifestyle-focused positioning, thus making the investment unjustified.
• Mini Market & Retail: We recommend relocating them from the reception area to prevent overcrowding and reduce visual clutter.
• Back-of-house areas: Incorporating approximately 20 sqm. of housekeeping pantries, one for every cluster of Ryads.
• Golf Carts & Service Vehicles: We suggest envisioning a dedicated area for lay-bys / parking, as well as charging stations.
