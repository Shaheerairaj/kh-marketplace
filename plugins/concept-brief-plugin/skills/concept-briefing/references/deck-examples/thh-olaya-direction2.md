VISION

MISSION

To create Riyadh’s most welcoming
lifestyle hospitality destination. A
community where modern travellers,
weekend explorers, and local residents
come together to experience a
refined and rooted sense of luxury.

To channel Riyadh’s spirit by
embracing local talent, craft, and
tradition in a way that feels both
grounded and forward-thinking.

We envision a place where work
blends effortlessly with leisure, where
wellness is a way of life, and where
timeless design and heritage come
together to tell a story that feels subtly
local and universally inviting.
Our vision is to lead the evolution of
hospitality in the Kingdom by setting a
new standard: one that is gracious yet
elevated, social yet serene, and
designed not just for staying, but for
belonging, connecting, and flourishing.

To infuse refined design, regional
artistry, and sensory experiences that
reflect the capital’s rich cultural fabric.
To cultivate meaningful connections
through spaces that invite gathering.
From community rituals and wellness
moments to storytelling, sharing, and
exchange.
To support modern professionals with
flexible, beautifully designed
workspaces that offer calm, clarity,
and a sense of place.

FOOD SOUK

Food Souk is a vibrant, all-day culinary destination
that reimagines the traditional food market
through a modern, curated lens. Inspired by
Mamlakah’s spirit of communal dining, it brings
together a rotating mix of chef-led stalls, concept
kitchens, and pop-up experiences creating a
social, ever-evolving hub for food lovers.
Designed for flexibility, Food Souk caters to every
rhythm of the day. Whether guests are grabbing a
quick bite between meetings, enjoying a casual
coffee catch-up, or settling in for a leisurely dinner.
With dine-in options, shared seating areas, and
convenient takeaway formats, it offers an
accessible, high-quality experience that fits
seamlessly into busy urban lifestyles.

SOCIAL HUB
2nd and 3rd Floors
Ouspace is a distinctive lifestyle, social, and business hub
designed to foster connection, knowledge-sharing, and
entrepreneurial growth. Set within an elegant, light-filled
environment defined by geometric patterns, natural stone
finishes, and warm neutral tones, it caters to the evolving
needs of the next generation of professionals, while offering
a serene and adaptable setting for focused work.
From private offices and hot desks to dedicated desks and
collaborative zones, the space supports both productivity
and privacy. Beyond daily work needs, Ouspace is also
equipped to host corporate events, conferences, workshops,
and banqueting functions, making it a versatile venue for
business gatherings and community engagement. Ouspace
is the ideal environment for work, networking, and highimpact events all within a design-forward space that
maximizes comfort and efficiency.

WELLNESS COMPONENT
THE GYM
20th Floor
The 20th floor at The House Hotel and Residence Olaya
is dedicated to wellness. A calm, dimly lit space shared
by both hotel and residential guests. Designed with
simplicity and flow in mind, it encourages quiet
connection across short- and long-stay communities.
The gym is arranged into discreet zones for cardio,
strength, and mobility, with soft lighting, pale wood
flooring, and neutral tones creating a focused,
distraction-free environment. A separate studio
supports yoga, stretching, and guided movement
sessions. The experience is serene, intentional, and
grounded in a philosophy of everyday well-being.

THE IMPORTANCE OF
ART COLLABORATIONS
The art that will be showcased in
the Cultural Hub at The House
Hotel & Residence Olaya offers
guests an inspiring pause - a
space to reflect, connect, and
discover. In-house guests can
explore the rotating exhibitions at
their leisure, while external visitors
can access the space through a
curated day-pass program.

The hub will feature works by
leading regional talents such as
Lulwah Al Homoud, known for her
intricate geometric abstractions;
Nasser Al Salem, whose
conceptual calligraphy explores
the intersection of language and
meaning; and Princess Reem AlFaisal, whose evocative blackand-white photography captures
the soul of Arab identity and
architecture. Together, these
exhibitions will transform the hub
into a living gallery. A refined,
ever-evolving reflection of the
region’s creative pulse.
