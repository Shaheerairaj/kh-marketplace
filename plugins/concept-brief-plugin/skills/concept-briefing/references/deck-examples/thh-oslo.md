VISION MISSION
To transform this landmark Oslo property into a vibrant
lifestyle destination where contemporary hospitality
meets the city’s cultural energy. The House Hotel Oslo
celebrates design, creativity, and community, offering
an experience that feels authentic and distinctly
Nordic.

To create a place where people experience Oslo in a
genuine way. Through thoughtful design, warm service,
and carefully curated experiences, The House Hotel will
connect guests and residents with the city’s culture,
creativity, and everyday rhythm.

THE ACCOMODATION

The House Hotel Oslo will feature 41 thoughtfully
designed guest rooms and suites set within a restored
landmark building in the heart of the city.
Each space will combine the character of the historic
architecture with contemporary Nordic interiors, creating
rooms that feel calm, refined, and comfortable. Natural
materials, generous layouts, and understated design will
shape an atmosphere that reflects Oslo’s sense of
simplicity and quality.

addresses.

THE WELLBEING

Beyond the facilities, the focus will be on programming

and reconnect.

HISTORICAL MAP SHOP

The space that celebrates the city’s heritage and spirit
of exploration will offer a curated selection of antique
and contemporary maps, prints, and books that reflect
Oslo’s history and its relationship with travel, nature, and
discovery.
More than a retail space, the shop will host small talks,
exhibitions, and collaborations with local historians and
collectors. This will create a unique cultural corner within
the hotel where guests and locals can engage with the
stories and landscapes that have shaped Oslo.

Dar Tantora The House Hotel showcases AlUla's rich artistic
heritage through its traditional murals, which adorn the hotel
walls and the wider Old Town. These murals, historically painted
as wedding gifts, depict local life, flora, fauna, and symbols of
happiness.
Architect Shahira Fahmy's team meticulously restored these
artworks, including those on wooden doors, in collaboration
with archaeological experts and local craftsmen. The Al-Dirah
Art School played a crucial role by researching and recreating
AlUla's traditional color palette using pigments sourced from
local rocks and plants, ensuring historical accuracy in the
restoration.
