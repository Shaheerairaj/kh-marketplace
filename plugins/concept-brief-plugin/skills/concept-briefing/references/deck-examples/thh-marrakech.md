Design & Identity
•

Modern, earthy, and quietly refined, never ornamental, never
themed.

•

Architecture that dissolves the boundary between indoors and
outdoors, embracing light, air, and landscape.

•

Clean lines. Natural palettes. Generous open spaces.

•

Each space carries one subtle Moroccan gesture, a carved
screen, a geometric shadow play, a tactile tile detail,
thoughtfully integrated rather than decorative.

•

No lantern clichés. No heavy mosaics.

•

More desert modernism than riad nostalgia, a contemporary
country estate with local soul.

•

Landscape design rooted in nature, movement, and sensory
wellbeing, gardens that invite stillness, shade, ritual, and
connection.

Opportunities
• A clear white space in Marrakech: the market is
saturated with ornamental riads and heritage luxury,
but lacks a contemporary, design-led lifestyle estate
• Taps directly into the accelerating demand for branded
lifestyle residences and regenerative wellness travel
• Strong ecosystem and clustering potential across
lifestyle concepts (Wellness studio + social café +
movement club + curated retail)
• Positions The House as a new benchmark for integrated
mixed-use living in Morocco
• A scalable concept with replication potential across
emerging resort markets
