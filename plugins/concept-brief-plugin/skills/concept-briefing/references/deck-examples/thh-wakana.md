The Kerten Hospitality team is truly excited to join forces with Wakana – a place of rare natural beauty,
deep ancestral roots, and extraordinary potential. We see this project not as a transformation, but as a
continuation: building on the remarkable work already done, while shaping a destination that will
resonate far beyond its borders.
In calling it Wakana The House Hotel, we honour the spirit and identity that has been cultivated over
the past 10 years – preserving its essence while infusing it with new energy, purpose, and global visibility.

We extend our gratitude to CBRE for the opportunity to present our proposition, and a special thanks to
Julia Catalán Diez alongside Ibervalles for the invaluable insights and the warm, comprehensive site
tour, which allowed us to gain a deeper understanding of both the project and the vision behind it.
We are confident that Kerten Hospitality can bring exactly the energy, creativity, and passion this
project deserves and elevate Wakana The House Hotel to the international stage it is destined for.

CONCEPT METHODOLOGY
Based on our understanding of the project, we built our concept on five key criteria to ensure
successful development and future operations:
1. Identity, Sustainability, and Opportunities
Our concept reflects Wakana’s and the region´s identity, blending the cultural authenticity
with sustainability while respecting the development´s opportunities and limitations
2. Target Markets & Guest Appeal
We focus on high-spending domestic and international guests, both leisure and corporate,
considering the property´s favourable accessibility and infrastructure to align offerings with
market demand
3. Competitive Landscape
A thorough market analysis helps us identify existing offerings and gaps, ensuring our product
stands out locally and on international scale
4. Value-Cost Balance
We strive for a balanced (I) cost/investment, (II) value creation, and (III) long-term returns to
maximize the property's future valuation.
5. Strengths of Kerten Hospitality
Leveraging from our expertise of creating destinations with innovative lifestyle driven brands
GOAL: CREATION OF SUSTAINABLE LONG-TERM VALUE

While the creative possibilities for Wakana are truly limitless, we believe it's essential that the design and
guest experience align with the expectations of today’s discerning traveler.
The overall concept, layout, and vision are strong and inspiring. However, we see an opportunity to
refine and elevate the design of the accommodation units and interiors to better resonate with a
higher-value guest segment – one that is very much present in this region. This is also in line with the
feeling from our site inspection and the project teaser’s description.
Importantly, this uplift remains true to Wakana’s natural, grounded spirit. It’s about enhancing comfort
and aesthetic quality in a way that supports the financial sustainability of the project while still honouring
its authenticity and soul.

Wakana The House Hotel is more than the sum of its parts – it is a cohesive ecosystem
where nature, community, and creativity converge to create a sustainable, yearround destination.
