# Section 2 — Positioning Thesis

**Purpose:** A single sentence that captures why Kerten is the right operator for this property and what the winning angle is. This becomes the spine of the entire proposal — everything downstream should be traceable back to it.

---

## Proposal Approach

Synthesise from Section 1 and any additional context in the conversation. A strong thesis names:

- The specific Kerten brand
- The market gap or opportunity
- The segment or location context
- The owner alignment or deal rationale

Do not make a generic claim about Kerten's capabilities. Push the user toward specificity at every iteration.

---

## Quality Bar

**Weak:** "Kerten brings lifestyle hospitality expertise to this market."

**Strong:** "Cloud 7's tech-forward lifestyle positioning fills a gap in Dubai Marina's midscale segment, where the owner's investment-focused priorities align with Kerten's asset-light management model."

The difference: the strong version names the brand, the gap, the segment, and the owner alignment in one sentence. If the user's version is missing any of these elements, point out what is absent and propose a revision that adds it.
