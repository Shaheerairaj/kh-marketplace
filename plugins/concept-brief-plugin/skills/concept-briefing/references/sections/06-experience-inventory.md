# Section 6 — Experience Inventory

**Purpose:** Translate the confirmed Concept Direction into a concrete operational programme — the F&B concepts, wellness offer, and signature experiences that will make this property distinctive and bookable. Do not begin this step until Step 3 (Concept Direction) is confirmed; everything here flows from the Vision and Pillars.

---

## Sub-sections

Work through these three sub-sections in order. Each has a different input source but all must be traceable back to the confirmed Pillars.

---

### 6a — F&B Concepts

**What it is:** The 1–3 named Kerten F&B brands recommended for this property, with a brief rationale for each selection.

**Proposal approach:**

- Read `references/kh-fnb-brands.md` to review the full F&B brand library
- Match brands to this property using three criteria: positioning tier, target guest profile, and the "Best fit for" field in each brand entry
- Propose only brands that genuinely fit — do not force a brand to fill a slot
- For each proposed brand, state which Pillar it serves and why it belongs at this property specifically
- If no existing brand fits a clearly needed F&B role (e.g. a rooftop bar concept for a property where none of the library brands apply), note the gap rather than forcing a misfit

**Typical combinations by property type:**

- Urban lifestyle hotel: Joontos (social anchor) + Ouspace (co-working)
- Urban lifestyle hotel with strong F&B identity: Charcood + Joontos
- Mixed-use with retail activation: Food Souk + Ouspace
- Coastal/beach resort: Nyssa Beach (primary) + one secondary concept
- High-footfall lobby or residential amenity: Nakhati (retail) as a complement to a primary dining concept

These are patterns from past deployments, not rules. The Concept Direction overrides any default combination.

---

### 6b — Wellness

**What it is:** The wellness offer appropriate to this property — its register, its anchors, and its connection to the overall concept.

**Proposal approach:**

- There is no standard Kerten wellness brand; the wellness concept is derived fresh for each property
- Use three inputs: the property type and physical context, the Target Guest profile from Step 3, and the Pillars
- Define the register first (restorative vs. active vs. longevity vs. social wellness) before proposing specific facilities or programming
- Name the 2–3 anchors of the wellness offer: what does a guest actually do here?
- Scan `references/deck-examples/INDEX.md` for comparable property types — past examples show the range: barefoot regenerative (Zanzibar), longevity clinic (THH Marrakech/Istanbul), alpine movement (Arlberg), communal hammam (THH Istanbul)

**Property context signals:**

- Beach / coastal → regenerative, nature-led, oceanfront ritual
- Alpine / mountain → movement, thermal, seasonal rhythm
- Urban → social wellness, longevity clinic, recovery-focused
- Heritage / cultural district → spa rooted in local ritual and craft (hammam, hydrotherapy, traditional treatments)

Avoid listing generic spa facilities (treatment rooms, steam, sauna) without a concept frame. The wellness offer should have a point of view.

---

### 6c — Signature Experiences

**What they are:** The 1–3 distinctive programmatic elements that give this property its memorability and word-of-mouth. Signature experiences are not standard hotel amenities — they are ownable, schedulable moments that a guest would cite when recommending the property.

**Proposal approach:**

- Derive directly from the Pillars confirmed in Step 3 — each Pillar should have at least one experiential expression
- Each signature experience should be: named, schedulable (it happens at a specific time or on a specific cadence), and tied to something concrete at this property (a space, a partnership, a heritage element)
- Scan `references/deck-examples/INDEX.md` for comparable properties and load 1–2 relevant examples — past signature experience formats are the strongest pattern reference:
  - Maker Souk (THH Marrakech) — live artisan residencies rotating quarterly
  - Alpine Atelier (Cloud 7 Arlberg) — cheese, craft, and wine programming tied to season
  - Historic Map Shop (THH Oslo) — unique retail as cultural anchor
  - Joontos rooftop (multiple) — social programming tied to a physical view
- Aim for 2–3 experiences. More than 3 dilutes the concept and signals lack of focus.
- Each experience should pass the "could only happen here" test — if it would work equally well at a Marriott across the street, it is not a signature experience.

---

## Sequencing note

Propose F&B first — it is the most concrete and the user can engage with named brands immediately. Wellness second — it requires more derivation and benefits from F&B being settled. Signature Experiences last — they are the most creative and the hardest, and they are easier to frame once F&B and wellness have established the property's register.
