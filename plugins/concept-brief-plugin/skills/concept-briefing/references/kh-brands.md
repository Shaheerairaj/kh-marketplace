# Hotel Brands

## The House Hotel (THHR)

**Positioning:** Boutique lifestyle hotel defining a new category of "Understated Contemporary Lux" — intimate, design-led, and rooted in the spirit of each location

**Tier:** Upper Upscale to Luxury

**Target guest:** Creative-industry professionals aged 30–45 — designers, architects, publishers, film producers; cosmopolitan design sophisticates who reject large chain hotels in favour of something personal and unique

**Design language:** Timeless, considered, and locally sensitive; buildings of architectural note with heritage and character; crafted interiors with fine detailing, local art, and a warm Mediterranean sensibility; never corporate, never generic

**Best fit for:** City-centre or culturally significant locations with strong local identity; buildings with heritage, architectural presence, or a story to tell; markets where discerning travellers seek boutique alternatives to international chains

**Guest personas:**

- **The Creative Professional** — Early 30s, works in fashion, design, or a related creative field. Single, driven, socially active. Wants a stylish base in a fashionable neighbourhood with a strong community feel. Brand-conscious and leads her social circle.

- **The Design-Savvy Couple** — Mid-30s, dual income, taste-driven rather than money-driven. City-breakers seeking a central location, great restaurant, spa access, and a genuine local experience. Laid back, adventurous, allergic to corporate hotels.

- **The Senior Creative on Assignment** — Mid-40s, film, media, or publishing. Extended stays while on location. Wants an interesting neighbourhood, gym, great food, and design that rewards attention. Confident, sociable, experience-led.

- **The Urban Resident** — Late 30s, professional, looking for a serviced apartment rather than a transient hotel stay. Wants to be in the right part of town, close to work, with concierge service and space to host. Culturally engaged, values experience over product.

## Cloud 7

**Positioning:** Local-loving, tech-forward, playful lifestyle hotel built for the next-generation traveller — genuine, stylish, and affordable

**Tier:** Midscale to Upper Midscale

**Target guest:** Curious, connected travellers of any age with a youthful mindset — fast-paced globe-trotters who want authentic experiences without paying luxury rates

**Design language:** Spontaneous, vibrant, and eclectic; colour palette shifts by environment (dark urban, terracotta for mountain, turquoise for coastal); open-plan social hubs replace formal reception; Cloud and "7" motifs adapted per property; sensory layering via music, scent, and dynamic lighting

**Best fit for:** Urban centres, secondary cities, and resort locations (coastal or mountain); new builds, conversions, or adaptive reuse; mixed-use developments where co-living, co-working, and hotel product can coexist

**Guest personas:**

- **The Connected Explorer** — Mid-20s to mid-30s, works remotely or in a creative/tech field. Travels frequently, values authenticity over luxury. Wants fast Wi-Fi, a social hub to work from, and local recommendations over generic hotel programming. Spends as much time in the lobby as in the room.

- **The Spontaneous Weekender** — Late 20s to early 40s, youthful mindset regardless of age. Short city breaks or quick getaways. Wants a stylish, affordable base that feels local — not a corporate chain. Drawn in by design, social energy, and a good F&B offer.

- **The Budget-Conscious Lifestyle Traveller** — Values design and experience but won't overpay for them. Midscale budget, upper-midscale expectations. Seeks genuine, well-considered spaces over bland standardisation. Cloud 7 is the brand that finally speaks to them in the midscale tier.

# Restaurant & F&B Brands

## Charcood

**Positioning:** Bold, unapologetic live-fire grill restaurant celebrating the primal art of charcoal cooking — "No distractions. Pure. Bold. No frills. Like a rough gem."

**Tier:** Casual Upscale / Premium Casual Dining

**Target audience:** Adventurous food lovers who appreciate craftsmanship and simplicity; curious connoisseurs, middle-aged diners, couples, and business lunches; younger foodies expanding their culinary horizons

**Design language:** Dark, warm, and minimalistic to brutalist; open kitchen as centrepiece — theatre of fire and smoke; rough, earthy materials; evening atmosphere shifts to a sophisticated, lively social vibe

**Best fit for:** Hotel F&B outlets or standalone urban sites; properties where an experiential, chef-driven concept is needed; evening-led venues that benefit from a strong culinary identity

---

## Food Souk

**Positioning:** A dynamic multi-brand F&B destination that brings multiple food concepts under one roof — part culinary market, part incubator for emerging "foodpreneurs"

**Tier:** Casual / All-Day Dining Destination

**Target audience:** Vibrant, food-curious urbanites; creative professionals who live an active, experience-driven lifestyle; broad demographic appeal through format diversity

**Design language:** Energetic, colourful, and market-inspired; adaptive by nature — spaces evolve with trends and tenant mix; zoned identity system per food counter within a unified envelope

**Best fit for:** High-footfall hotel lobbies, mixed-use developments, or retail destinations where variety and dwell time are the objective; properties with space for multiple F&B stalls; markets where a single restaurant concept would underperform but a diverse food hall would thrive

---

## Joontos

**Positioning:** A vibrant shared-dining destination blending gastropub accessibility with Michelin-level culinary craft — "Live · Explore · Share"

**Tier:** Casual Upscale / Experiential Dining

**Target audience:** Educated, affluent, sociable food enthusiasts aged 21–50 — young professionals, foodies, and socialites seeking a communal dining experience

**Design language:** Warm, lively, and community-driven; designed for sharing — social tables, bar areas, event and music space; locally inspired with international culinary references; approachable yet refined

**Best fit for:** Urban hotel F&B or standalone sites in high-footfall lifestyle districts; properties targeting a social, younger-affluent guest mix; locations where a branded F&B anchor would drive destination traffic

---

## Nakhati

**Positioning:** Premium artisanal gelato sweetery blending authentic Italian craftsmanship with flavours rooted in local culture — "Handmade with Passion"

**Tier:** Premium Artisanal Retail / Specialty F&B

**Target audience:** Urban millennials and young professionals seeking unique, high-quality treats; families; health-conscious consumers; supporters of female entrepreneurship and local community

**Design language:** Feminine and bold; earthy, warm interiors with wood, marble, brass, and wicker; hand-drawn ink illustrations; colour palette of dark rose, olive green, and ocean turquoise; logo references Arabic calligraphy and henna

**Best fit for:** Hotel lobby retail, high-street premium locations, mall kiosks, or cart deployments; franchise-scalable across store, kiosk, and cart formats; markets where locally rooted, artisanal F&B concepts are valued

---

## Nyssa Beach

**Positioning:** A laidback-chic beach club where life is lived at its best — blending entertainment, leisure, wellness, and seasonal dining into an all-day coastal experience

**Tier:** Premium Beach Club / Lifestyle F&B

**Target audience:** Adults aged 25–50 seeking serenity and escape; wellness-conscious professionals, health-aware individuals, families and couples who appreciate nature, quality, and sustainability

**Design language:** Calm, natural, and graceful; rooted in the mythology of Nysa — nurturing, elegant, water-connected; earthy and organic materials; ambience shifts through the day from relaxed mornings to lively sunset gatherings

**Best fit for:** Coastal or lakeside hotel properties and resort destinations; standalone beachfront sites; properties targeting wellness and leisure guests; locations where an all-day F&B and lifestyle programme adds significant value

---

## Ouspace

**Positioning:** A flexible, fully serviced co-working and business lifestyle concept that transforms underutilised hotel or residential space into a productive community hub — "Redefining Workspaces"

**Tier:** Co-Working / Business Lifestyle Concept

**Target audience:** Entrepreneurs, startups, freelancers, remote workers, corporate hybrid teams, digital nomads, and students

**Design language:** Fresh, functional, and flexible; dynamic open layouts; comfort-driven furnishings and calming aesthetics; localised cultural touches per market

**Best fit for:** Cloud 7 and House Hotel properties where underutilised space can be activated; residential buildings seeking a business amenity; standalone sites in business districts; cities with high startup or remote-worker density

## UBBU *(Not a brand — ESG Initiative)*

UBBU is Kerten Hospitality's Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) framework, not a guest-facing brand. It commits to environmental sustainability, community integration, and local economic empowerment — embedding local culture, artists, and businesses into all KH operations. The UBBU platform is patent-pending and will be licensable to third-party hospitality operators.
