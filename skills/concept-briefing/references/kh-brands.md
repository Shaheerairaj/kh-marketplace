# Kerten Hospitality — Brand Reference

Use this reference when proposing Brand Fit Hypothesis (Section 1) and Positioning Thesis (Section 2), and whenever brand alignment is relevant to the conversation.

---

## Cloud 7

**Positioning:** Tech-forward lifestyle hotel for the connected generation
**Tier:** Midscale to Upper Midscale
**Target guest:** Young professionals, digital nomads, tech-savvy leisure and business travellers
**Design language:** Clean, modular, smart-room integrated. Co-working spaces and social common areas are core to the product.
**Differentiators:** Smart room technology, high-speed connectivity infrastructure, co-working and event spaces embedded into the hotel programme, community-driven F&B
**Best fit for:** Urban locations with high young-professional or startup density, secondary city markets underserved by lifestyle brands, properties where the owner wants a brand with clear tech differentiation

---

## The House Hotel

**Positioning:** Boutique luxury with a residential soul
**Tier:** Upscale to Upper Upscale
**Target guest:** Design-conscious travellers, boutique luxury seekers, guests who prefer intimate scale over grand hotel formality
**Design language:** Residential feel, curated interiors, locally anchored. Each property has a distinct identity rather than a standardised look.
**Differentiators:** Intimate scale, design-led identity, personalised service model, strong F&B programming with local character
**Best fit for:** Boutique conversions, heritage buildings, markets where travellers seek authenticity over chain-hotel consistency, deals where the owner values brand prestige at a boutique scale

---

## HOSME

**Positioning:** Social hospitality at the intersection of hotel and home
**Tier:** Midscale
**Target guest:** Experience-driven travellers, long-stay guests, communities seeking connection over transactions
**Design language:** Warm, communal, flexible. Spaces designed for mixing — guests, locals, and residents share the same environment.
**Differentiators:** Community programming, hybrid stay formats (short and extended stay), social F&B and event concepts, neighbourhood integration
**Best fit for:** Mixed-use developments, urban neighbourhoods with strong local culture, properties targeting a blend of short-stay and extended-stay demand
